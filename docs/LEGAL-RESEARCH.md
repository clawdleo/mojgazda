# Legal Requirements for Anonymous Employer Review Platform in Slovenia and the Balkans

*Compiled: February 3, 2026*

## Executive Summary

This legal brief addresses the key requirements for launching an anonymous employer review platform (similar to Glassdoor) in Slovenia and the broader Balkans region, with focus on legal protections, GDPR compliance, platform liability laws, and business registration requirements.

**Key Findings:**
- EU Digital Services Act (2022) provides strong platform liability protections for hosting services
- GDPR allows anonymous reviews with proper data minimization and transparency
- Slovenia follows EU e-Commerce Directive hosting safe harbors
- D.o.o. registration is straightforward with €7,500 minimum capital requirement
- No Section 230 equivalent, but DSA and eCommerce Directive provide comprehensive protections

---

## 1. Platform Liability Protections and Defamation Defense

### EU Legal Framework

**Digital Services Act (2022)** - The primary legal protection framework:
- Applies to all online services operating in the EU, including Slovenia
- **Hosting Safe Harbor** (similar to Section 230): Platforms not liable for user-generated content if they:
  - Don't have actual knowledge of illegal content
  - Act expeditiously to remove content when notified
  - Don't actively moderate or editorialize content
- **Notice and Takedown System**: Must respond to flagged illegal content
- **Transparency Requirements**: Must explain content removals and provide appeals process

**e-Commerce Directive (2000/31/EC)** - Still in effect alongside DSA:
- **Article 14 Hosting Safe Harbor**: Platform not liable for stored information if:
  - Provider doesn't have actual knowledge of illegal activity
  - Provider acts expeditiously to remove upon obtaining knowledge
  - Provider doesn't play an "active role" in moderating content

### Practical Protection Strategy

1. **Maintain "Passive Host" Status**: 
   - Don't editorially modify reviews
   - Use automated moderation tools only
   - Avoid manual curation that shows "active role"

2. **Implement Robust Notice & Takedown**:
   - Clear reporting mechanisms for defamatory content
   - Response within 24-48 hours to legal takedown requests
   - Detailed logging of all moderation actions

3. **User Terms of Service**:
   - Prohibit defamatory content
   - Require users to base reviews on personal experience
   - Include indemnification clauses

## 2. GDPR Requirements for Anonymous Reviews

### Data Minimization and Anonymization

**Article 5 GDPR - Data Minimization Principle**:
- Collect only data necessary for platform operation
- Anonymous reviews require minimal personal data
- Can store: email (for verification), IP address (for fraud prevention)
- Cannot store: Full names, detailed personal information

**Article 6 - Lawful Basis**:
- **Legitimate Interest** (Article 6(1)(f)) for platform operation
- **Consent** for optional features (email newsletters, etc.)

### Anonymous Data Handling

**Recital 26 GDPR - Anonymous Information**:
- "Information which does not relate to an identified or identifiable natural person"
- Once truly anonymized, GDPR doesn't apply
- Key: Ensure reviews cannot be traced back to individuals

**Practical Implementation**:
- Hash email addresses with salt
- Don't store IP addresses long-term
- Use pseudonymous IDs for reviews
- Separate user accounts from review content

### Transparency Requirements

**Articles 13-14 GDPR - Privacy Notice Must Include**:
- What data is collected (email, IP address, review content)
- Why it's collected (platform operation, fraud prevention)
- How long it's stored (see retention schedule below)
- User rights (access, deletion, portability)

## 3. Slovenian E-Commerce and Platform Liability Laws

### Zakon o elektronskem poslovanju (Electronic Commerce Act)

Slovenia has implemented the EU e-Commerce Directive into national law through its Electronic Commerce Act. Key provisions:

**Hosting Services Protection**:
- Follows EU Article 14 hosting safe harbor exactly
- No general monitoring obligation for platforms
- Must act expeditiously on notice of illegal content

**Information Requirements**:
- Must display company registration details
- Contact information for users and authorities
- Terms of service in Slovenian language

**Notice and Takedown Procedure**:
- No specific national procedure defined
- EU best practices apply
- Must maintain records of takedown actions

### Enforcement and Penalties

**Supervisory Authority**: Slovenian Data Protection Commissioner
- Handles GDPR compliance
- Can impose fines up to 4% of annual turnover

**Content Disputes**: Local courts handle defamation claims
- Platforms protected under hosting safe harbor
- Individual users remain liable for defamatory reviews

## 4. Legal Structure Protection from Defamation Claims

### Recommended Corporate Structure

**Družba z omejeno odgovornostjo (d.o.o.) - Limited Liability Company**:
- **Limited Liability**: Personal assets protected
- **Professional Management**: Clear separation of company and personal liability
- **EU Jurisdiction**: Access to EU legal protections

### Additional Protection Layers

1. **Professional Liability Insurance**:
   - Coverage for legal defense costs
   - Recommended coverage: €1-5 million
   - Include cyber liability coverage

2. **Terms of Service Protection**:
   - User indemnification clauses
   - Limitation of liability provisions
   - Choice of jurisdiction (Slovenia)

3. **Content Moderation Policies**:
   - Clear community guidelines
   - Proactive removal of obviously defamatory content
   - Maintain hosting safe harbor status

## 5. EU Platform Liability: DSA vs. "Section 230"

### Digital Services Act (2022) - EU's Answer to Section 230

**Broader than Section 230**:
- Covers all online platforms, not just in US
- Includes proactive risk assessment requirements
- Stronger transparency obligations

**Key Differences from US Section 230**:
- **Good Faith Moderation**: DSA doesn't require "good faith" efforts
- **Algorithmic Transparency**: Large platforms must explain content ranking
- **Risk Assessment**: Platforms >45M users must assess systemic risks
- **External Auditing**: Independent risk audits required

**Practical Advantages**:
- No "publisher vs. platform" distinction confusion
- Clear safe harbor for hosting services
- EU-wide consistency across 27 countries

## 6. Right to be Forgotten vs. Review Integrity

### GDPR Article 17 - Right to Erasure

**When Right to be Forgotten Applies**:
- Personal data no longer necessary
- Consent is withdrawn
- Data processed unlawfully
- Legal obligation to erase

**When Platform Can Refuse Erasure**:
- **Article 17(3)(a)**: Exercise of freedom of expression and information
- **Article 17(3)(e)**: Public interest, scientific or historical research
- **Article 17(3)(f)**: Legal claims establishment or defense

### Balancing Strategy

**Review Content**: 
- Generally protected as freedom of expression
- Can refuse erasure if review serves public interest
- Must anonymize rather than delete when possible

**User Account Data**:
- Must allow deletion of personal account information
- Can maintain anonymized review content
- Use data minimization to reduce deletion obligations

**Practical Implementation**:
1. Separate user profiles from review content
2. Allow profile deletion while preserving anonymous reviews
3. Provide clear explanation when refusing review deletion
4. Document public interest justification for each refusal

## 7. Slovenia D.o.o. Registration Requirements

### Registration Process and Requirements

**Through AJPES (Agency for Public Legal Records)**:
- **Minimum Share Capital**: €7,500 (as of 2026)
- **Registration Timeline**: 1-2 weeks for standard registration
- **Registration Cost**: ~€400-600 (notary fees, court fees, publication)

**Required Documents**:
- Memorandum of Association
- Articles of Association 
- Proof of share capital deposit
- Director's consent and eligibility proof
- Registered office confirmation

### Ongoing Compliance Requirements

**Annual Obligations**:
- Annual financial statements to AJPES
- Tax returns (corporate tax: 19%)
- VAT registration if turnover >€50,000
- Social security for employees

**Corporate Governance**:
- Must have at least one director
- Annual shareholders meeting required
- Maintain corporate records

### Operating Costs (Annual)

- **Accounting**: €2,000-5,000
- **Legal compliance**: €1,000-3,000  
- **Corporate taxes**: 19% on profits
- **Total estimated annual compliance**: €5,000-10,000

## 8. Regional Laws: Croatia, Serbia, and Balkans

### Croatia - EU Member State

**Legal Framework**:
- Full EU law compliance (GDPR, DSA, eCommerce Directive)
- Zakon o elektroničkoj trgovini (Electronic Commerce Law)
- Same protections as Slovenia

**Business Registration**:
- D.o.o. (similar to Slovenian d.o.o.)
- Minimum capital: €2,500
- Registration through FINA

### Serbia - EU Candidate State

**Legal Framework**:
- Law on Electronic Commerce (aligns with EU Directive)
- Personal Data Protection Law (GDPR-aligned)
- No DSA implementation yet

**Hosting Protections**:
- Similar to EU hosting safe harbor
- Notice and takedown procedures
- Limited liability for platforms

**Business Registration**:
- D.o.o. registration
- Minimum capital: €500
- Registration through Business Registers Agency

### Regional Strategy

**Single EU Entity Approach**:
- Register d.o.o. in Slovenia
- Operate across EU under single registration
- Gradual expansion to Serbia/Bosnia as they approach EU membership
- Use hosting safe harbor protections consistently

## Risk Assessment

### High Risk Areas

1. **Defamation Claims**: Major risk but mitigated by hosting safe harbor
2. **GDPR Violations**: Fines up to 4% of revenue
3. **Cross-Border Enforcement**: Complex jurisdictional issues

### Medium Risk Areas

1. **Content Moderation Errors**: Over/under moderation risks
2. **Data Breaches**: Reputational and legal consequences
3. **Platform Growth Requirements**: DSA obligations for large platforms

### Low Risk Areas

1. **Corporate Liability**: Well-protected through d.o.o. structure
2. **EU Market Access**: Strong legal framework supports platform business
3. **Regional Expansion**: Clear legal pathway exists

## Conclusion

The EU legal framework, particularly the Digital Services Act and GDPR, provides a robust foundation for operating an anonymous employer review platform. Slovenia offers an advantageous combination of strong platform protections, reasonable compliance costs, and EU market access.

The recommended approach is to establish a Slovenia d.o.o., implement strong GDPR compliance from day one, maintain hosting safe harbor protections through passive content policies, and gradually expand to other Balkan markets as they continue EU integration.

Total estimated legal setup and first-year compliance costs: €40,000-60,000, representing a reasonable investment for accessing the entire EU market with strong legal protections.
